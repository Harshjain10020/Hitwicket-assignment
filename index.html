Assignment Question 2

Play Hitwicket for at least an hour over two days. Now, suggest a Feature to reduce churn in the first 15 min of gameplay. How will you go about arriving at a solution? Think out of the box.

Proposed Solution: "Redeem All" & Notification Hub

Observation: Daily rewards are cluttered in different locations. My Dad (50) spent half his time clicking red dots instead of playing the game. This breaks the "flow" immediately after the tutorial.

The Evidence: UI Clutter

During testing, I noticed users were overwhelmed by the sheer number of notifications.

As seen in the screenshot, there are "Claim" indicators (Red Dots) scattered across Store, Quests, Events, and Daily Goals simultaneously.

  • × Too many clicks to start playing

  • × Feels like "Admin Work", not a game

Assignment Question 3

Play Hitwicket for at least an hour over two days. Now, Design a Feature to get a user to login everyday for the first 7 days. Think out of the box.

Proposed Solution: The "Rookie Rivalry Arc"

Observation: Players want to attack their friends and express themselves (like in Bitmoji). The current system feels a bit lonely in the first week.

Figure B: Concept for "Doodle Captain Creator" & Rivalry Screen.

The Vision: Personalization

Users (especially the 16-22 age group) loved the Doodle art style but wished they could make the player look like them.

I propose adding a "Doodle Creator" where they can upload or build a Bitmoji-style avatar. We can then use this avatar to "Taunt" friends in the Co-op mode.

  • ✓ Higher Emotional Investment
  • ✓ Viral Sharing Potential ("Look at my captain!")
  • ✓ Perfect reward for Day 7 Login

Implementation: 7-Day Journey

Instead of a boring "Day 1 Bonus", we give them a specific Rival (Bot or Player). If they beat this Rival over 7 days, they win a cool prize.

Day 1

The Challenge

"Captain X thinks you're a fluke. Prove him wrong."

Days 2-6

The Battle

Daily tasks to beat the Rival. Attack Friends feature unlocks here.

Day 7 Prize

Upload Your Art

Unlock ability to upload your own Doodle/Bitmoji as the captain.

Future Expansion: Team Play

Adding a feature where 2-3 players can play together (Co-op mode) to challenge strong AI teams.

Assignment Question 4

Present a SWOT analysis of Hitwicket as a market opportunity in one slide.

Proposed Solution: SWOT Analysis

Comparing Hitwicket to WCC2, Sachin Saga, and Nazara games based on user feedback and market research.

My Strategic Find

I researched how other games increase engagement. A study on "Hitwicket AR Adventure" highlighted using Player Personas (Socializer vs Strategist). I've integrated this thinking belowâ€”we can't just build features; we need to build for specific personas.

Strengths (What we do well)

  • • Doodle Art Style: Unique visual identity vs 3D clones.
  • • Strong Backing: Backed by Harsha Bhogle & recognized by Tim Cook (High Credibility).
  • • Strategic Depth: % Prediction rate appeals to thinkers, not just clickers.

Weaknesses (What we need to fix)

  • • No Bowling: Alienates "Sim" purists who want manual control.
  • • Social Depth: Missing deep social structures like "Guilds" or "Clans" that keep PUBG players loyal.
  • • UI Clutter: Messy dashboard causes friction (Q2).

Opportunities (Where to grow)

  • • Benchmarking PUBG: Introduce "Cricket Clubs" (Clans) and an "Elite Pass" (Battle Pass) for monetization.
  • • Tech Innovation: Experiment with AR (Augmented Reality) events to bring the stadium to the living room.
  • • Team Play: 2-3 Player Co-op mode.
  • • Bitmoji: Personalized player Bitmoji.

Threats (Watch out for)

  • • Feature Fatigue: Competitors like Nazara are adding "Meta" layers fast. If we don't add social depth, users may leave.
  • • Real Money Gaming: Betting apps during IPL steal attention.
